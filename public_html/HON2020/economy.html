Economy Build

Worldbuilding: Game of Thrones - Build 3

    In a production sense, the Hidden Isle has little to no production made out of the land. Due to its status as a very urban city center, there are little to no resources - thus most of their resources must be imported. They are able to stay this way due to the impact their hidden location has on their political power throughout the world, allowing them to acquire resources by visiting other areas of the world rather than having the goods brought to them. As mentioned in the geography build, their discovery of steam engine technology allows them to be incredibly efficient when importing goods without having to reveal their location The economy of the Hidden Isle is based strongly around their production of goods and using their superior technology to and specialized labor to create goods for the rest of the world. The basis of this superior technology comes from their in depth knowledge of the sciences found throughout the world, as explained in the history build. Additionally, the entrance of kids into school at their birth leads to an economy where everyone is an expert in something, decided at a young age based on their skills and talents. As people age, society treats them similar to the elders in the Ancient Greek city of Sparta, where they retire and live in the city but are allowed to run for council positions to work with those selected to rule the government through the school system. These retirees are also the center of entrepreneurship in the city, as once they have filled the obligation of their specialization they can pursue whatever they want through the use of their capital and labor.
    Due to its lack of natural resources, the Hidden Isle imports all of the food that it consumes. With their adoption of steam engines, however, they are able to have a constant influx of food, so there are never any shortages or crises regarding food. Most of their clothing is developed in the city through specialists, but there is also clothing that has been imported. Shelter is found through the professional system, where people in the same specialty all live in houses together since there is no concept of family. In regards to wants, the population has their own personal life outside of their professional life, but their professional life is very rigid based on the career path that they are placed on. Some career paths have more of a time committment compared to others, and thus they have much less time for wants. The shortage of natural resources causes the Hidden Isle to be very dependent on importing goods, and the specialized economy causes them to have a surplus of improved goods through labor and capital.
    The Hidden Isle works under a command economy, where the government sets all of the rules. This can be seen through their schooling system, where all of the kids are sent to school immediately, and in their professional system, where specializations are decided for the citizens based on their strengths and weaknesses. One big benefit to this is that there is no unemployment, as everyone is given a specialization by the government. That being said, depending on the usefulness of their profession, different citizens are paid different amounts in the form of stars, the local currency on the hidden island. These stars are coins surrounded by pointy edges, and are minted by the goverment and circulated into the local economy through government purchases. Citizens are then able to use these stars in their personal life to satisfy any needs or wants. While there is a central treasury run by the government to keep the city prosperous, there is no banking system, as there is no need for debt in the command economy where everyone's profession is decided from a young age.
